Shumai Bento

焼売弁当 (shumai bentō)

Shumai bento is a delightful Japanese lunchbox featuring tender, bite-sized dumplings known as shumai. These dumplings are typically made with a delicate mixture of ground pork, shrimp, and seasonings, wrapped in a thin dough wrapper, and then steamed to perfection. Shumai is cherished for its savory flavor and juicy texture, making it a beloved choice in Japanese cuisine. Alongside the shumai, the bento usually includes a bed of steamed rice, pickled vegetables such as radish or cucumber, and a side of dipping sauce, often soy-based with a hint of sweetness and acidity. Shumai bento offers a delightful combination of flavors and textures, making it a satisfying and convenient meal option enjoyed at home, work, or on the go.



Ingredients Recipe
  • 1 cup cooked rice (note 1)
  • 5 pieces Shumai (note 2)
  • ½ portion Iri Tamago from Sanshoku Bento recipe
  • ½ portion Peas from Sanshoku Bento recipe
  • 3 round slices of carrot cut into flower shape (3mm (⅛") thickness, Note 3)
  • 2 tbsp Sweet and Sour Pickled Red Cabbage
  • 1 okazu cup
  1. Fill about half of the bento box with the rice, allowing for 1cm / ⅜” margin at the top (see the first photo in this post).
  2. Place Shumai pieces next to the rice, filling most of the remaining space of the bento box.
  3. Put Pickled Red Cabbage in the okazu cup and place it next to the shumai.
  4. Cover half of the rice with Iri Tamago and the rest with Peas.
  5. Place the flower-shaped carrots over the line between the egg and the peas.
Recipe Notes
  • It is best to pack the cooked rice in a bento box while hot or warm as it is easier to shape the rice into the bento box.
  • Shumai can be made ahead and you can even freeze them. If frozen, reheat them in the microwave or a steamer before packing.
  • If your bento box is larger or the amount of rice you filled the bento box with is smaller, you may increase the number of Shumai pieces.
  • If you have a vegetable cutter in a flower shape (see the post Sanshoku Bento for samples), that would be the easiest. But if you don't, you can still make a flower carrot with a knife.
  • To make a plum flower shape with a knife: Slice one round slice (3mm / ⅛" thickness) taken from the length of the carrot. Make 5 small v-shaped cuts around the edge of the round slice in equal spacing. Shape the edges of the petals by removing pointy edges.
  • Boil or microwave the flowers to soften them.
