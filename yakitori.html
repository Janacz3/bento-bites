Yakitori Bento

焼き鳥弁当 (yakitori bentō)

Yakitori bento is a delightful Japanese lunchbox meal featuring skewered and grilled pieces of tender, flavorful chicken known as yakitori. This dish embodies the essence of Japanese street food culture with its savory, smoky flavor and juicy texture. The yakitori chicken is typically seasoned with a soy sauce-based marinade or a savory tare sauce, lending a rich umami taste to each bite. Served alongside a bed of steamed rice, yakitori bento often includes a variety of accompaniments such as pickled vegetables, sesame seeds, and sometimes a small salad or side dish. The combination of grilled chicken skewers and traditional Japanese flavors makes yakitori bento a popular and satisfying choice for lunch or a casual meal on the go.



Ingredients Recipe
  • 1 cup Shimeji Gohan (note 1)
  • 4 skewers Yakitori (note 2)
  • 1 Pickled Chrysanthemum Turnip (note 3)
  • 2-3 tbsp Gomoku-mame (Simmered Soybeans with Vegetables)
  • 3 broccoli florets , blanched
  • 2 mini tomatoes
  • A bento box of your choice (I used a double decker bento box)
  • 1 okazu cup (note 4)
  1. While the Shimeji Gohan is still hot or warm, place it in one section of the bento box and let it cool.
  2. Place the turnip next to the Gomoku-mame. If you don’t have a partition between the rice and the turnip, place the turnip in an okazu cup so that the rice does not get vinegary.
  3. Place tomatoes and broccoli florets in one side of the other bento box.
  4. Place yakitori skewers next to the vegetables.
Recipe Notes
  • It is best to pack cooked rice in a bento box while hot or warm. This makes it easier to shape the rice into the bento box.
  • I used chicken thigh Yakitori (momo). You can pick and choose any kind of Yakitori such as negima (chicken thigh and shallots/scallions).
  • The skewers that I used in my Yakitori recipe were too long to fit in my bento box. So, I used teppōgushi which are shorter with a flat handle. Please visit my post Yakitori for more details about skewers.
  • Please refer to my recipe Pickled Chrysanthemum Radish. To add an extra colour to the bento, I used turnip, but you can use radish instead.
  • See my post for more details about okazu cups. You can use cupcake cases or aluminium foil instead.
  • Depending on the number of partitions in your bento box, you may need more okazu cups, e.g. for the pickled turnip.
